01 — The Bassoon's Spectral Fingerprint
A Fourier transform decomposes any periodic sound into a stack of simple sine waves — harmonics — each at a whole-number multiple of the fundamental frequency. The bassoon on C3 (130.8 Hz) has a distinctive profile: the fundamental is moderate, harmonics 2–5 dominate, then the spectrum rolls off gradually. This gives the instrument its dark, reedy warmth.
